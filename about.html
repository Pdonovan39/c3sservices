Our story
Built from scratch.
Grown through trust.

C3S was founded in March 2015 by Curtis Zweier — a software engineer and engineering executive with over 25 years of experience leading development teams across enterprise software companies in the Philadelphia area.

Curt had spent years on both sides of the outsourcing equation — hiring outsourcing firms as a VP of Development, and watching most of them fall short. Too little transparency. Too little ownership. Too little accountability. He believed there was a better way to build and run remote engineering teams, and C3S was his proof of concept.

We started with one client and two engineers. That first client is still with us today. Not because of a long contract — but because the team earned it.

2015
Founded in Philadelphia

C3S launched with one US client and two engineers in Cairo. The focus: build a team that outperforms in-house employees.

2016–19
Building the foundation

Early client wins in cybersecurity, enterprise SaaS, and electronics distribution. First clients are retained through renewals — not contracts.

2020–22
Scaling through the pandemic

Remote-first by design, C3S continued delivering without disruption while the rest of the industry scrambled to adapt.

2023–24
Expanding into AI engineering

C3S begins building production AI for enterprise clients — autonomous agents, enterprise chatbots, and deep workflow integrations.

2025
Zero turnover. Forward-deployed.

0% engineer turnover. AI agents in production. 300+ engineers serving US companies across 15+ industries — and still growing.

What we stand for
Our values aren't on a poster.
They're in how we work.
Quality without compromise

We don't cut corners to hit deadlines. We flag problems before they become yours, push back when something isn't right, and hold ourselves to a standard that goes beyond “it works.” This is why clients stay with us for years — not months.

Full transparency, always

You see everything — sprint velocity, code quality, delivery health, team structure. We don't hide problems or spin bad news. Transparency is what makes a remote team feel like your team.

Ownership over execution

Our engineers don't just close tickets. They care about the product, ask why before they build, and take responsibility for outcomes — not just outputs. This is the difference between a contractor and a team member.

Retention over replacement

We invest in our engineers — their growth, their working conditions, and their sense of pride in what they build. In 2025, our turnover was zero. That's not a coincidence. It's the result of building a team people actually want to be part of.
