Our story
Built from scratch. Grown through trust.

C3S was founded in March 2015 by Curtis Zweier — a software engineer and engineering executive with over 25 years of experience leading development teams across enterprise software companies in the Philadelphia area.

Curt had spent years on both sides of the outsourcing equation — hiring outsourcing firms as a VP of Development, and watching most of them fall short. Too little transparency. Too little ownership. Too little accountability. He believed there was a better way to build and run remote engineering teams, and C3S was his proof of concept.

We started with one client and two engineers. That first client is still with us today. Not because of a long contract — but because the team earned it.

Over 11 years we've grown to serve US companies across 15+ industries, placing 300+ engineers and building a culture that prioritizes quality, ownership, and retention above everything else. In 2025, our engineer turnover was zero.

What we stand for
Our values aren't on a poster.
They're in how we work.

Three principles that have guided every hiring decision, every client engagement, and every line of code we've written since 2015.

Quality without compromise
We don't cut corners to hit deadlines. We flag problems before they become yours, push back when something isn't right, and hold ourselves to a standard that goes beyond "it works." This is why clients stay with us for years — not months.
Full transparency, always
You see everything — sprint velocity, code quality, delivery health, team structure. We don't hide problems or spin bad news. Transparency is what makes a remote team feel like your team.
Ownership over execution
Our engineers don't just close tickets. They care about the product, ask why before they build, and take responsibility for outcomes — not just outputs. This is the difference between a contractor and a team member.
Our culture
A team that stays because they want to — not because they have to

C3S has an unusually strong retention rate for a software company. Our engineers stay because they're treated like professionals, given real ownership, and work on challenging problems that matter. Curt founded C3S in 2015 after more than two decades leading engineering teams at enterprise software companies in the Philadelphia area. As VP of Development at NTP Software and Director of Development at DefendX Software, he spent years both hiring outsourcing firms and watching them fail — too little transparency, too little ownership, too little accountability.

He started C3S to do it differently. The model was simple: build a remote engineering team that performs better than a client's own employees, treats engineers like professionals, and earns trust through quality rather than contracts. Eleven years later, that first client is still with us.

Curt holds an MS in Computer Science from Drexel University and a BS in Computer Science, also from Drexel. He remains deeply involved in client relationships and the strategic direction of C3S.
